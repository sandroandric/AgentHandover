Your agents don't know
how you work.

AgentHandover runs locally on your Mac, watches your screen through a vision model, and turns your real workflows into step-by-step procedures that Claude Code, OpenClaw, or any agent can follow. You never write an instruction. You just work.

screen capture → vision model annotation → activity classification → task continuity → semantic alignment → procedure generation → human review → agent handover

Not screen recording

Screen recording gives you pixels. AgentHandover gives you structured understanding — app context, step sequences, CSS selectors, form field IDs, typed variables, verification criteria, and failure recovery. All from watching you work. All deletable. All local.

Trust nothing by default

Every procedure passes through a 7-state lifecycle with human approval at every gate. Screenshots are deleted after annotation. Passwords are never captured. 3 execution failures auto-demote a procedure. The system is designed to be honest about what's ready — it will never tell an agent something works when it doesn't.

Private by architecture

All AI runs on your machine via Ollama. No cloud. No telemetry. No accounts. Artifacts encrypted with XChaCha20-Poly1305. Raw events auto-purge after 14 days. Nothing leaves your laptop.

$ agenthandover doctor
$ agenthandover setup --vlm
$ agenthandover start all
